Scaffolding costs in Essex vary depending on the size of the property, the type of scaffold required, how long it needs to be in place and any access challenges (such as narrow driveways, adjacent roads or neighbouring properties).

As a rough guide for South Essex:

  • Small residential job (e.g. chimney repoint, single-storey extension) — typically £300–£600
  • Standard 2–3 bed house (e.g. full roof, render or fascia replacement) — typically £600–£1,200
  • Larger detached or 4-bed property — typically £1,200–£2,500+
  • Commercial projects — priced individually based on height, footprint and duration

These prices include erection, hire for an agreed period and dismantling. The best way to get an accurate figure is a free no-obligation quote from our Rayleigh team — call 01702 820468 or use our online quote form.

Erection time depends on the size and complexity of the scaffold:

  • Small residential jobs (e.g. chimney, single elevation) — 3–4 hours
  • Standard 2–3 bed house (full perimeter or roof) — half a day to one full day
  • Larger or more complex structures — 1–2 days
  • Commercial projects — 2–5 days depending on scope

Once your quote is approved, most South Essex jobs are booked within 2–5 working days. For urgent situations, call 01702 820468 — we offer rapid-response emergency scaffolding across Rayleigh, Southend, Basildon and surrounding areas.

Yes — every operative at Axis Scaffolding Essex holds a current CISRS card (Construction Industry Scaffolders Record Scheme), the recognised UK standard for scaffolding competency.

UK law (the Work at Height Regulations 2005) requires that any work at height must be carried out in the safest reasonably practicable way. For most roofing, rendering, fascia, chimney or multi-storey work, scaffolding is legally required — a ladder alone is not sufficient to give tradespeople a safe working platform.

If your builder or roofer needs to spend more than a few minutes working at height, or needs to carry tools and materials, scaffolding is almost certainly needed. When in doubt, ask us — we'll give you an honest assessment at no charge.

Yes — if scaffolding overhangs or sits on a public highway, pavement or road, a licence from the local council (a Section 169 licence under the Highways Act 1980) is required before erection. This applies in Rayleigh, Southend, Basildon, Chelmsford and across all Essex councils.

We are experienced in handling these requirements. We advise all clients who need highway scaffolding to apply to their local authority in advance — processing can take 5–14 working days. We can guide you through what's needed. Call 01702 820468 to discuss.

Temporary roofing is a weatherproof scaffold structure built over a property to protect it from rain, wind and elements while roof works are carried out. It essentially creates a covered working environment so your project can continue whatever the British weather does.

You'll typically need temporary roofing when:

  • A full roof strip or replacement is taking place over multiple days
  • The existing roof has been damaged and the interior needs protecting urgently
  • Works are planned for autumn or winter when weather delays are likely

We provide temporary roofing across South Essex including Rayleigh, Southend, Basildon and Canvey Island. Call 01702 820468 for a free quote.
